## Why the original cohort crossed 10x

Turn zero was tied between WKVM and the strongest working vLLM setup found for
the original cohort at that time: 40.265 versus 40.469 seconds. Concurrency
does not make that one-time prompt
computation 10x faster. It changes the continuation regime:

| Engine | Mean continuation barrier |
|---|---:|
| WKVM | 2.982 s |
| vLLM | 41.945 s |
| SGLang | 98.179 s |

WKVM keeps all 16 compact states resident. vLLM exposes 213,205 KV tokens,
or 5.14 complete 41,456-token requests. SGLang clamps its requested pool to
56,936 tokens, about 1.37 final histories. At B16, both full-KV engines must
queue requests and repeatedly lose long prefixes, while WKVM advances every
resident state.

The earlier eight-turn fair cohort was 5.109x versus vLLM and 12.009x versus
SGLang for the complete session. Extending a genuinely long-lived agent
session to 48 predeclared turns amortizes the tied cold turn and measures the
resident-state advantage over the remaining 47 barriers.

## Cold-path audit

A B16 x 36,864 one-turn, one-output-token scout measured 37.375 seconds and
isolated the prompt path from the 64-token decode tail. A separate
`CUDA_LAUNCH_BLOCKING=1` attribution run measured 49.497 seconds and assigned
15.896 seconds to MLP, 15.448 seconds to cache update, and 9.353 seconds to
attention. Its inflated wall is diagnostic only.

Authoritative closing-chunk cache updates were changed to retain homogeneous
B2 batching. The production eight-turn A/B completed in 60.382 seconds,
inside the prior 59.506--60.821-second range, so this change is a bounded
cleanup rather than the reason the 48-turn gate passes.

For fixed eight-turn BF16 10x versus vLLM, the measured 19.462-second WKVM
continuation portion leaves only 11.612 seconds for turn zero. Incremental
scheduler, HTTP, or cache-write changes cannot credibly reduce the current
roughly 40-second turn zero to that bound. A fixed-short-session claim needs a
different cold path, such as quality-gated FP8 compared fairly with FP8
incumbents, or a separately disclosed precomputed-state workload.

## Incumbent configurations

The original cohort used the strongest memory-safe fast-prefill configuration
found at that time in the local 0.24.0 build:

```text
--kv-sharing-fast-prefill
--compilation-config mode=0,cudagraph_mode=FULL_DECODE_ONLY
--gpu-memory-utilization 0.82
--max-num-batched-tokens 4096
--max-model-len 41456
```

SGLang 0.5.14 used BF16 Triton attention, full decode graphs, 2,048-token
chunked prefill, 16 requested running requests, and a 0.94 static-memory
fraction. It has no equivalent Gemma shared-tail fast-prefill mode in this
build.

## Claim limits

This is strong exploratory evidence, not publication-grade evidence:

- one paired cohort rather than three;
- dirty worktree: 30 tracked and 43 untracked paths at launch;
- desktop prelaunch baseline around 2,933--2,935 MiB rather than a headless
  baseline below 1 GiB;
- WKVM uses approximate routed-span semantics while incumbents use full KV;
- one GPU, one model, one workload shape.

The later mode-3 audit shows that even the scoped 10x-vLLM result is no longer
valid. Publication requires a newly frozen fastest-incumbent campaign with
three paired clean-tree, headless repeats and must keep the complete workload
and semantic wording in the claim.
